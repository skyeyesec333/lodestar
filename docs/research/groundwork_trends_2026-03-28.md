# Groundwork Research Memo

Date: March 28, 2026

## EXIM / project finance signals

- EXIM's structured and project finance program says it has supported more than 83 projects in 36 countries with more than $37 billion in loans, and it continues to frame project finance as a multi-contract, due-diligence-heavy product that depends on balanced risk allocation and substantially final agreements. Source: EXIM project finance guidance and approach pages.
- FY2025 project-finance authorizations on EXIM's transactions page show three authorized project-finance transactions totaling $1.24 billion:
  - Guyana Gas-to-Energy, Guyana, oil and gas, $527 million
  - Polskie Elektrownie Jadrowe, Poland, nuclear power, $25 million
  - Pengerang Energy Complex, Malaysia, petrochemicals, $688 million
  This shows the active mix is not just renewables; it spans energy, nuclear, petrochemicals, and strategic industrial capacity.
- On January 30, 2026, EXIM announced a credit agreement with Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe under the Engineering Multiplier Program for Poland's first nuclear project. That is a clear signal that early-stage technical-services financing and pre-FID work are part of the opportunity surface, not just final asset financing.
- On April 30, 2025, EXIM gave its Deal of the Year award to the Guyana Gas-to-Energy Project, reinforcing Guyana and energy infrastructure as a visible live use case.
- On October 20, 2025, EXIM announced seven Letters of Interest totaling more than $2.2 billion for critical-minerals projects in Australia, many under the EXIM-Export Finance Australia Single Point of Entry structure. This is important because it extends the opportunity set beyond classic host-country infrastructure into allied supply-chain resilience and multi-agency coordination.
- EXIM's critical-minerals page shows how policy is expanding:
  - CTEP flexibility
  - MMIA support for domestic export-linked manufacturing and infrastructure
  - export-nexus flexibility for critical-minerals projects
  - support ranging from mines to gigafactories

## SaaS market signals

- Capital-project SaaS is converging around integrated systems plus AI assistance:
  - Oracle Primavera Cloud positions itself as a shared planning, scheduling, resource, and risk platform for owners and delivery teams.
  - Autodesk Construction Cloud is pushing Autodesk AI and assistant workflows directly inside construction operations.
  - InEight launched "InEight Intelligence" on October 13, 2025, explicitly positioning AI as advisory support for capital-project delivery.
- Regulated financial-workflow SaaS is converging around AI-powered intelligence inside secure workflow systems:
  - Intapp DealCloud markets itself as an AI-powered deal and relationship intelligence platform for regulated markets, with workflow configurability, zero-entry activity capture, document ingestion, and AI summaries.
  - Datasite continues to push AI-enhanced diligence workflows, integrated Q&A, trackers, audit trails, and AI redaction for sensitive transaction environments.
- The pattern is consistent across categories: buyers want AI embedded in workflow, not a separate chatbot, and they want strong governance, auditability, and domain structure.

## Repeated bottlenecks

- No sponsor-centric operating layer exists for the pre-close phase of EXIM-style deals.
- Current systems split across:
  - capital-project execution tools
  - data rooms
  - CRMs / relationship tools
  - meeting transcription tools
  - spreadsheets for readiness and conditions tracking
- EXIM requires feasibility, market, environmental, contractual, sponsor, supplier, and off-take evidence in substantially final form. The work is cross-document and cross-party, but current software is mostly single-surface.
- Allied / multi-agency structures create coordination complexity that generic PM and VDR tools do not model well.
- Strategic-industrial and supply-chain cases create additional evidence burdens:
  - U.S. content
  - export nexus
  - engineering / FEED progression
  - policy-program fit

## White-space implications for Groundwork

- The strongest positioning is not "project management for infrastructure."
- The strongest positioning is:
  - sponsor operating system for export-finance readiness
  - evidence-backed submission readiness
  - cross-party commitment and condition tracking
  - narrative consistency across feasibility, market, business-plan, and financing materials
- The best wedge is before financial close, where:
  - current capital-project tools are too delivery-centric
  - current deal tools are too origination-centric
  - current data rooms are too passive
- Groundwork should treat the EXIM / ECA checklist as the structured spine, then organize meetings, stakeholders, documents, conditions, and supply-chain / policy evidence around it.
