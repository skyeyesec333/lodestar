## 2. Program and policy motion

- EXIM is broadening beyond a narrow overseas infrastructure frame into domestic export-linked manufacturing and strategic supply chains through MMIA and critical-minerals pathways.
- Allied-ECA coordination is becoming more important, especially where Export Finance Australia and EXIM are explicitly coordinating.
- Program fit itself is becoming operational work: sponsors increasingly need to evidence U.S. content, export nexus, engineering maturity, and policy relevance, not just commercial viability.

## 3. Deal-flow intelligence

- Active EXIM project-finance activity is cross-sector rather than mono-sector.
- FY2025 visible project-finance transactions span Guyana gas-to-energy, Poland nuclear engineering, and Malaysia petrochemicals.
- The practical lesson for Lodestar is that the product should model common financing-readiness patterns, not assume one single project archetype.

## 4. Workflow bottleneck map

- Readiness visibility:
  - Sponsors rarely know their true submission state before external diligence starts.
- Commitment tracking:
  - Meeting outputs do not naturally become durable obligations tied to evidence and milestones.
- Cross-document consistency:
  - Feasibility, market, business-plan, engineering, and financing materials drift apart easily.
- Counterparty qualification:
  - EPC, off-taker, and obligor readiness lives in fragmented notes and spreadsheets.
- Condition management:
  - Funders, agencies, and advisors create blocker sets that are not managed in one system of record.

## 6. Buyer and value map

- Primary buyers:
  - independent developers
  - project-finance advisors
- High-value collaborators:
  - EPC BD teams
  - legal and technical consultants
  - institutional partners in later stages
- Economic trigger:
  - delayed or failed readiness before LOI / preliminary commitment / diligence
- Measurable value:
  - fewer missed blockers
  - faster assembly of submission-grade evidence
  - lower coordination cost across sponsors and advisors
  - less narrative drift across critical documents

## 7. White-space conclusions

- The market is not missing generic AI.
- The market is missing a sponsor-side operating system for financing readiness before financial close.
- The moat is the structured domain model:
  - readiness ontology
  - evidence chain
  - counterparty and condition graph
  - cross-document consistency layer

## 8. Implications for Lodestar

- Keep the initial wedge focused on EXIM-first sponsor readiness.
- Design the system around evidence, conditions, commitments, and artifacts rather than generic tasks or folders.
- Treat AI as an operating layer inside a trusted system of record, not as the core product claim.
- Build program-specific logic as overlays on durable concepts so category expansion is possible later without rewriting the spine.
