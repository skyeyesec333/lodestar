# US EXIM Bank Glossary for Lodestar

Comprehensive glossary of US Export-Import Bank terms for project finance.
All definitions verified against official exim.gov sources fetched March 2026.

---

TERM: Export-Import Bank of the United States (EXIM)
ACRONYM: EXIM
DEFINITION: The official export credit agency (ECA) of the United States. EXIM provides direct loans, loan guarantees, export credit insurance, and working capital guarantees to support U.S. exports. It is an independent federal agency whose mission is to support American jobs by facilitating the export of U.S. goods and services.
CONTEXT: EXIM is the primary counterparty for all Lodestar-tracked projects. Every project in the platform is pursuing EXIM financing, making EXIM's requirements, policies, and timelines the governing framework for the entire readiness score.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/about/eligibility
RELATED: ECA, DFC, OECD Arrangement
RECENT_CHANGES: Reauthorized through 2026 with expanded CTEP mandate and Make More in America initiative.
---

TERM: Direct Loan
DEFINITION: A fixed-rate loan made directly by EXIM to a foreign buyer or financial intermediary to purchase U.S. exports. Direct loans use the CIRR (Commercial Interest Reference Rate) as the base lending rate, calculated as the applicable U.S. Treasury rate plus 100 basis points. EXIM offers direct loans for medium-term (up to 7 years, amounts up to $10M) and long-term (over $10M) transactions.
CONTEXT: In project finance, direct loans are one of two primary EXIM financing structures (alongside loan guarantees). The fixed-rate nature provides certainty for financial modeling, and the CIRR-based pricing is often more competitive than commercial rates for emerging market borrowers.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/our-approach-to-project-finance
RELATED: CIRR, Loan Guarantee, PEFCO, Disbursement
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Loan Guarantee
DEFINITION: A guarantee by EXIM covering the repayment of a loan made by a commercial lender to a foreign buyer of U.S. exports. EXIM guarantees the lender against non-payment by the borrower due to both commercial and political risks (comprehensive cover) or political risks only. The guaranteed loan rate is negotiated among borrower, guarantor, and lender rather than set by EXIM.
CONTEXT: Loan guarantees are the most common EXIM financing structure for large project finance transactions. They allow commercial banks to participate in emerging market lending they would otherwise decline, and the guarantee terms directly affect the project's cost of capital.
SOURCE: https://exim.gov/resources/applications-forms/letter-interest/guidelines-for-letters-interest
RELATED: Direct Loan, Comprehensive Cover, Political-Only Cover, Exposure Fee
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Working Capital Guarantee
ACRONYM: WCGP
DEFINITION: An EXIM guarantee providing 90% loan-backing to a commercial lender extending working capital financing to a U.S. exporter. The guarantee covers both revolving and transaction-specific facilities, enabling exporters to borrow against export-related accounts receivable (up to 90% advance rate) and export-related inventory (up to 75% advance rate). There is no minimum or maximum transaction amount.
CONTEXT: While not directly part of the project finance data room, the WCGP is relevant for U.S. EPC contractors and equipment suppliers participating in EXIM-financed projects who need working capital to fulfill export orders.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/working-capital
RELATED: Export Credit Insurance, Supply Chain Finance Guarantee
RECENT_CHANGES: EXIM offered 100% coverage under the Underserved Markets Program for eligible exporters.
---

TERM: Export Credit Insurance
DEFINITION: Insurance policies issued by EXIM that protect U.S. exporters against losses from foreign buyer non-payment due to commercial or political risks. Coverage extends up to 95% of sales invoices. EXIM offers multiple policy types including Single Buyer, Multi-Buyer, Letter of Credit, and Medium-Term policies, each tailored to different export scenarios.
CONTEXT: Export credit insurance enables U.S. suppliers to offer competitive credit terms to foreign buyers. For project finance sponsors, insured receivables improve the borrowing base and can facilitate bridge financing during the project development phase.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/export-credit-insurance
RELATED: Political Risk, Commercial Risk, Working Capital Guarantee
RECENT_CHANGES: Launch of Equity Express Select Export Credit Insurance for underserved businesses.

TERM: Letter of Interest
ACRONYM: LOI / LI
DEFINITION: A non-binding indication from EXIM of its willingness to consider financing a given export transaction on specified terms. The LOI is issued based on a limited review and does not constitute a commitment to lend. The non-refundable processing fee is $100 ($1,000 for a Competitive LOI). An LOI is valid until its stated expiry date, with one 12-month extension available for a maximum total validity of two years.
CONTEXT: The LOI is the first formal EXIM milestone in Lodestar's project lifecycle. Obtaining an LOI demonstrates that EXIM has reviewed the project at a preliminary level and found it potentially eligible. It is a prerequisite for advancing to Preliminary Commitment and is often required by other project stakeholders as proof of financing viability.
SOURCE: https://exim.gov/resources/applications-forms/letter-interest/guidelines-for-letters-interest
RELATED: Preliminary Commitment, Final Commitment, Preliminary Information Memo
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Preliminary Commitment
ACRONYM: PC
DEFINITION: A non-binding preliminary offer of EXIM financing terms issued following a credit evaluation and policy review. The PC indicates that the borrower's needs generally meet EXIM's financing requirements, subject to contract award and additional review. It represents a stronger indication of EXIM support than an LOI but is not yet a binding authorization.
CONTEXT: The PC is a critical de-risking milestone for project sponsors. It signals that EXIM has conducted substantive due diligence and found the project creditworthy. Many project sponsors use the PC to finalize negotiations with EPC contractors, off-takers, and equity investors.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/loan-guarantee/application-process
RELATED: Letter of Interest, Final Commitment, Board of Directors
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Final Commitment
ACRONYM: AP (Authorization to Proceed)
DEFINITION: The binding authorization by EXIM's Board of Directors to provide financing for a specific transaction. Final Commitment follows receipt of a final commitment application, review by EXIM staff, and board action. It requires that the export contract has been awarded and all EXIM conditions, policies, and legal requirements are satisfied. The commitment fee begins accruing 60 days after AP authorization on undisbursed amounts.
CONTEXT: Final Commitment is the definitive financing approval in the EXIM process and a key milestone in Lodestar. After AP, the project can proceed to financial close and first disbursement. All contracts must be in substantially final form before the board will authorize AP.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/loan-guarantee/application-process
RELATED: Letter of Interest, Preliminary Commitment, Financial Close, Commitment Fee
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Substantially Final Form
DEFINITION: EXIM's documentation standard requiring that all key project agreements (EPC contracts, off-take agreements, concession agreements, etc.) be near-executed versions rather than term sheets, summaries, or outlines. Contracts must be sufficiently complete that only minor, non-material changes remain before execution.
CONTEXT: This standard is central to Lodestar's readiness scoring. Documents in "substantially final form" score higher than drafts or outlines, and EXIM will not issue a Final Commitment without key agreements meeting this threshold. It is one of the most common reasons projects stall in the application process.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/our-approach-to-project-finance
RELATED: Final Commitment, Data Room, Readiness Score, EPC Contract
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Country Limitation Schedule
ACRONYM: CLS
DEFINITION: EXIM's country-by-country listing that identifies nations where EXIM financing is restricted, limited to certain coverage types, or entirely unavailable. The CLS distinguishes between countries eligible for comprehensive cover (commercial + political risk), political-only cover, and countries where no EXIM support is available. Completely restricted countries include Afghanistan, Belarus, Bolivia, Burma, Cuba, Iran, Libya, North Korea, Russia, Sudan, Syria, and Venezuela, among others.
CONTEXT: The CLS is a threshold eligibility check in Lodestar. If the project's host country is restricted, the project cannot proceed with EXIM financing. For countries with political-only limitations, the project structure must account for the narrower coverage, affecting risk allocation and pricing.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/about/eligibility
RELATED: Political-Only Cover, Comprehensive Cover, Political Risk
RECENT_CHANGES: Russia and Belarus added to fully restricted list following 2022 sanctions.

TERM: Comprehensive Cover
DEFINITION: The broadest form of EXIM guarantee or insurance coverage, protecting the lender or exporter against both commercial risk (buyer inability or unwillingness to pay) and political risk (government actions, currency inconvertibility, war, etc.). Comprehensive cover is available only in countries not restricted to political-only cover under the CLS.
CONTEXT: Projects with comprehensive cover have lower residual risk for lenders, resulting in better financing terms. The availability of comprehensive cover is a key input to the project's financial model and risk allocation structure.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/our-approach-to-project-finance
RELATED: Political-Only Cover, Country Limitation Schedule, Exposure Fee
RECENT_CHANGES: None known
---

TERM: Political-Only Cover
DEFINITION: A narrower form of EXIM guarantee that covers only political risks -- government expropriation, currency inconvertibility, political violence, and breach of government undertakings -- but excludes commercial risks such as buyer default or insolvency. Political-only transactions receive a 10% discount to the Minimum Premium Benchmark (MPB) on exposure fees.
CONTEXT: In some host countries, EXIM offers only political-only cover per the CLS. Sponsors pursuing projects in these markets must separately address commercial risk, often through additional credit enhancements or higher equity contributions. The 10% MPB discount partially offsets the narrower protection.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/fees-for-project-finance
RELATED: Comprehensive Cover, Country Limitation Schedule, Exposure Fee, MPB
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Exposure Fee
DEFINITION: The risk premium EXIM charges for accepting credit risk on a guaranteed or insured transaction. The fee is calculated based on the buyer's risk profile, country risk classification, coverage percentage, repayment terms, and disbursement profile. For project finance, indicative minimum exposure fees range from approximately 6.5% to 22.7% of the financed amount, depending on tenor and whether the project earns hard currency. EXIM provides an online Exposure Fee Calculator for estimates, though actual fees are determined upon application approval.
CONTEXT: The exposure fee is a major cost component in the project's financial model. It is typically paid upfront or capitalized into the loan, directly affecting the all-in cost of EXIM financing. Hard-currency-earning projects benefit from lower fees through Permitted Exceptions.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/fees-for-project-finance
RELATED: Minimum Premium Benchmark, Permitted Exception, Credit Classification, Commitment Fee
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Commitment Fee
DEFINITION: A fee charged by EXIM on the undisbursed portion of an authorized loan or guarantee. For project finance transactions with pre-completion coverage, the commitment fee is 0.5% (1/2 of 1%), with an additional 1/8 of 1% for each additional take-out option. For transactions without pre-completion coverage, the fee is 1/8 of 1%. The fee begins accruing 60 days after Final Commitment (AP) authorization.
CONTEXT: The commitment fee incentivizes timely drawdowns and affects the project's carrying costs during construction. Since it accrues on undisbursed amounts, construction delays directly increase this cost, making schedule adherence financially material.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/fees-for-project-finance
RELATED: Exposure Fee, Final Commitment, Disbursement, IDC
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Minimum Premium Benchmark
ACRONYM: MPB
DEFINITION: The minimum exposure fee floor established by the OECD Arrangement, effective since April 1, 1999. The MPB is based on country risk classification (seven categories) and ensures that export credit agencies do not undercut each other on pricing. The total nominal value of all fees charged as a percentage of the EXIM-financed amount (excluding the exposure fee itself) must equal or exceed the MPB.
CONTEXT: The MPB constrains EXIM's pricing flexibility and ensures a level playing field among OECD export credit agencies. Projects in higher-risk countries face higher MPBs, directly affecting the economics of EXIM financing versus alternative sources.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/fees-for-project-finance
RELATED: Exposure Fee, OECD Arrangement, Permitted Exception, Credit Classification
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Permitted Exception
DEFINITION: A discount to the Minimum Premium Benchmark available for project finance transactions that meet specific structural criteria. The most relevant exception for limited-recourse projects involves offshore future flow structures combined with offshore escrow accounts. Discount levels are: 20% for meeting all escrow criteria; 30% if LLCR averages at least 1.75:1 or 9 months debt service is pre-funded; 40% if LLCR averages at least 2.5:1 (or 2.0:1 with ADSCR at least 1.0:1), 12 months debt service is pre-funded, first-priority escrow interest, and over 80% private ownership.
CONTEXT: Permitted Exceptions can significantly reduce the exposure fee, making EXIM financing more competitive. Projects earning hard currency (e.g., oil and gas) with strong coverage ratios and offshore escrow structures benefit most, while local-currency-earning infrastructure projects (e.g., power plants) typically cannot access these discounts.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/fees-for-project-finance

RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: US Content
DEFINITION: The portion of an export contract's value that originates in the United States, calculated as the aggregate contract price minus eligible foreign content. EXIM finances the lesser of 85% of the net contract price or 100% of the U.S. content. For Transformational Export Area transactions under CTEP, full OECD-compliant financing is available if the transaction contains at least 51% U.S. content. All PRC-sourced content is deemed ineligible under CTEP. Material changes in foreign content exceeding 5% of contract value must be reported to EXIM.
CONTEXT: US content is a fundamental eligibility criterion and directly determines the maximum amount EXIM will finance. The Exporter's Certificate documenting content percentages is a required application document. Projects with higher US content can access more EXIM financing.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/policies/content/medium-and-long-term
RELATED: Eligible Foreign Content, Exporter's Certificate, CTEP, Net Contract Price
RECENT_CHANGES: CTEP expanded the 51% threshold for transformational export areas; all PRC content excluded.
---

TERM: Eligible Foreign Content
DEFINITION: Foreign-origin goods that are shipped from the United States to the foreign buyer as part of the export contract. Eligible foreign content may comprise up to 15% of the contract value and is included in the calculation of EXIM-supported amounts. Goods merely transshipped through the U.S. (including bonded warehouse entry) are excluded and cannot count as eligible foreign content.
CONTEXT: Understanding the distinction between eligible and ineligible foreign content is critical for structuring the EPC contract to maximize EXIM financing. Components sourced abroad but assembled or shipped from the U.S. may qualify, while transshipped goods do not.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/policies/content/medium-and-long-term
RELATED: US Content, Net Contract Price, Exporter's Certificate
RECENT_CHANGES: None known
---

TERM: Exporter's Certificate
DEFINITION: A certification document submitted by the U.S. exporter to EXIM that details the aggregate U.S. content and foreign content percentages of the export contract. The certificate documents local costs and content breakdowns, and EXIM uses it to calculate the maximum financeable amount. Material changes in foreign content exceeding 5% of contract value must be reported and will adjust future disbursements.
CONTEXT: The Exporter's Certificate is a required document in the EXIM application and a key item in the Lodestar data room checklist. Accuracy is critical because EXIM adjusts disbursements based on certified content ratios.
SOURCE: https://exim.gov/resources/applications-forms/letter-interest/guidelines-for-letters-interest
RELATED: US Content, Eligible Foreign Content, Disbursement
RECENT_CHANGES: None known
---

TERM: Local Costs
DEFINITION: Goods and services produced and procured within the host country of the project. EXIM supports local costs up to 30% of the U.S. contract value for standard project finance transactions, and up to 40-50% for certain transactions. Local costs must be included in the Export Certificate to qualify for EXIM support and must represent capital costs (not operating expenses).
CONTEXT: Local cost financing is essential for projects that rely on host-country labor, materials, or services during construction. The 30% ceiling affects project structuring -- sponsors must ensure that the local-cost share of the EPC contract stays within EXIM limits or find alternative financing for the excess.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/eligibility-of-costs
RELATED: US Content, Eligible Costs, EPC Contract, CAPEX
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: CAPEX (Capital Expenditure)
ACRONYM: CAPEX
DEFINITION: The total capital cost of constructing the project, including equipment, EPC services, engineering, legal, financial, and advisory services. EXIM supports only capital costs in project finance transactions; operating expenditures (O&M), working capital, owner's contingencies, pre-development/feasibility studies, and development fees charged by sponsors with ownership stakes are ineligible.
CONTEXT: CAPEX defines the total financing requirement and the base against which EXIM's coverage percentages apply. Sponsors must clearly separate eligible capital costs from ineligible items in their financial models to determine the EXIM-financeable portion.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/eligibility-of-costs
RELATED: Eligible Costs, Local Costs, US Content, EPC Contract
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: IDC (Interest During Construction)
ACRONYM: IDC
DEFINITION: Interest that accrues on disbursed loan amounts during the construction period before the project generates revenue. EXIM allows interest accrual financing during construction as part of its project finance flexibility, but interest cannot be capitalized after the completion date. IDC is a key cost item in the project's sources and uses of capital.
CONTEXT: IDC is a significant financing cost that must be modeled in the project's financial plan. Since EXIM allows IDC financing during construction, it is included in the total EXIM-financed amount, but the prohibition on post-completion capitalization means the project must begin servicing interest immediately upon completion.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/our-approach-to-project-finance
RELATED: CAPEX, Disbursement, Commitment Fee, Grace Period
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio)
ACRONYM: DSCR
DEFINITION: The ratio of a project's available cash flow to its total debt service obligations (principal plus interest) in a given period. EXIM requires that project sensitivity analysis demonstrate a sufficient DSCR to ensure uninterrupted debt servicing for the full term of the debt. While EXIM does not publish a single minimum DSCR threshold, its Permitted Exception discounts reference ADSCR levels of at least 1.0:1 as a benchmark.
CONTEXT: DSCR is the primary creditworthiness metric in EXIM project finance. Sponsors must demonstrate adequate DSCR under base, optimistic, and pessimistic scenarios in their financial model. Higher DSCRs can also qualify the project for exposure fee discounts through Permitted Exceptions.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/our-approach-to-project-finance
RELATED: LLCR, ADSCR, Financial Model, Sensitivity Analysis, Permitted Exception
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: LLCR (Loan Life Coverage Ratio)
ACRONYM: LLCR
DEFINITION: The ratio of the net present value of project cash flows available for debt service over the remaining life of the loan to the outstanding debt balance. EXIM references LLCR in its Permitted Exception discount tiers: an LLCR averaging at least 1.75:1 qualifies for a 30% discount; an LLCR of at least 2.5:1 (or 2.0:1 combined with ADSCR of at least 1.0:1) qualifies for a 40% discount on the Minimum Premium Benchmark.
CONTEXT: LLCR is a forward-looking creditworthiness measure that captures the project's ability to repay debt over the full loan tenor. Strong LLCRs directly reduce financing costs through exposure fee discounts.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/fees-for-project-finance
RELATED: DSCR, ADSCR, Permitted Exception, Exposure Fee
RECENT_CHANGES: None known
---

TERM: ADSCR (Annual Debt Service Coverage Ratio)
ACRONYM: ADSCR
DEFINITION: The annualized ratio of cash flow available for debt service to total debt service obligations in a given year. EXIM references ADSCR in its Permitted Exception criteria, requiring a projected ADSCR of not less than 1.0:1 in conjunction with LLCR thresholds for the highest discount tier (40%). The LLCR and ADSCR definitions must be agreed upon among OECD Arrangement participants.
CONTEXT: ADSCR provides a period-by-period view of debt service capacity, complementing the forward-looking LLCR. Both ratios must be modeled in the project financial plan and are monitored throughout the life of the EXIM exposure.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/fees-for-project-finance

TERM: Tenor
DEFINITION: The total duration of a loan from first disbursement to final maturity, including any grace period. For EXIM project finance, the maximum repayment term is 14 years under extended terms, with a maximum grace period of 2 years. Standard maximum average life is 5.25 years, extendable to 7.25 years with constraints. A 20 basis point premium applies if the tenor exceeds 12 years. Specific sectors have different maximums: up to 18 years for nuclear power plants and certain renewable energy and water projects.
CONTEXT: Tenor directly affects the project's debt service profile and the exposure fee calculation. Longer tenors provide lower annual payments but increase total interest cost and exposure fees. Tenor negotiations are a key part of structuring EXIM financing.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/our-approach-to-project-finance
RELATED: Grace Period, Repayment Profile, DSCR, Exposure Fee
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Grace Period
DEFINITION: The period after first disbursement during which the borrower is not required to make principal repayments, though interest typically continues to accrue. EXIM allows a maximum grace period of 2 years under extended terms for project finance. Principal repayment typically begins six months after the exporter's contractual responsibility concludes (i.e., after project completion/commercial operation date).
CONTEXT: The grace period aligns debt service with the project's revenue generation timeline. Since construction-phase projects generate no revenue, the grace period prevents cash flow mismatches during build-out.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/our-approach-to-project-finance

TERM: Disbursement
DEFINITION: The actual release of loan funds from EXIM to the borrower or directly to the exporter/supplier. Disbursements are made proportional to the certified U.S. content ratio across all shipments. Progress payments require supplier certification that accrued costs for actual work performed equal or exceed the payment value. Disbursements cannot exceed 60% of export value before shipment without Engineering and Environment Division approval.
CONTEXT: Disbursement mechanics affect the project's cash flow during construction and the timing of commitment fee and IDC accruals. The 60% pre-shipment cap and content-proportional disbursement rules must be modeled in the project's sources and uses schedule.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/eligibility-of-costs
RELATED: Commitment Fee, IDC, US Content, Exporter's Certificate, Progress Payment
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: CIRR (Commercial Interest Reference Rate)
ACRONYM: CIRR
DEFINITION: The official lending rate for export credit agency direct loans, calculated monthly based on government bond yields in the domestic market. EXIM's CIRR is the average U.S. Treasury rate from the previous month plus 100 basis points. Three tiers apply based on repayment period: 3-year Treasury for terms up to 5 years, 5-year Treasury for 5-8.5 years, and 7-year Treasury for terms exceeding 8.5 years. Rates are published on the Monday before the 15th of each month and become effective on the 15th.
CONTEXT: CIRR is the base rate for all EXIM direct loans. Since it is fixed at commitment, it provides interest rate certainty for the project's financial model. The CIRR-plus-100bp formula means EXIM rates track U.S. Treasury markets with a fixed spread.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/resources/commercial-interest-reference-rates/about-cirr-rates
RELATED: Direct Loan, Tenor, Interest Rate
RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Credit Classification
ACRONYM: CC
DEFINITION: EXIM's internal risk rating system for buyers and borrowers, used to determine exposure fee pricing. EXIM employs five distinct CC levels for private buyers, ranging from lowest to highest risk. Non-financial institutions and financial institutions are assessed under separate CC definitions. Medium-term, long-term, and Financial Institution Buyer Credit (FIBC) transactions use buyer-specific credit classifications, while short-term single-buyer policies continue to use country-based risk ratings.
CONTEXT: The credit classification assigned to a project's borrower directly determines the exposure fee, which is one of the largest cost components of EXIM financing. Sponsors should understand what CC level their project company is likely to receive to accurately model financing costs.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/resources/exposure-fees
RELATED: Exposure Fee, MPB, Country Risk
RECENT_CHANGES: Transition from country-based to buyer-specific risk assessment for most products.

TERM: EPC Contract (Engineering, Procurement & Construction)
ACRONYM: EPC
DEFINITION: A turnkey construction contract under which a contractor designs, procures equipment, and builds the project facility. EXIM requires EPC contracts to be "Date Certain" with adequate liquidated damages provisions for completion delays. The EPC contractor must demonstrate technical, managerial, and financial capability with three years of audited financial statements. For EXIM eligibility, the EPC contractor's work must contain sufficient U.S. content (ideally over 51% for CTEP transactions).
CONTEXT: The EPC contract is typically the largest single agreement in the EXIM data room. It defines the project's CAPEX, construction schedule, and technology. EXIM requires EPC contracts in substantially final form before Final Commitment, and the US content of the EPC work determines the EXIM-financeable amount.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/guidelines-for-submitting-successful-application

RECENT_CHANGES: None known

TERM: Off-Taker
DEFINITION: The entity contractually obligated to purchase the project's output (electricity, water, minerals, etc.) under a long-term agreement. EXIM requires off-takers to be creditworthy and the off-take agreements to be long-term contracted arrangements. The off-taker's credit quality is a key input to the project's cash flow reliability assessment.
CONTEXT: The off-taker's creditworthiness and the terms of the off-take agreement are among the most critical factors in EXIM's credit evaluation. A weak or non-creditworthy off-taker can prevent a project from obtaining EXIM financing. The off-take agreement must be in substantially final form for Final Commitment.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/guidelines-for-submitting-successful-application

TERM: PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)
ACRONYM: PPA
DEFINITION: A long-term contract between an electricity generator (the project company) and a power purchaser (typically a utility or government entity serving as off-taker), specifying the terms for the sale of electricity including price, volume, duration, and payment mechanisms. For EXIM project finance, PPAs should demonstrate revenue predictability and be denominated in or indexed to hard currency to mitigate devaluation risk.
CONTEXT: The PPA is the primary revenue contract for power generation projects, which constitute a large share of EXIM's project finance portfolio. EXIM evaluates the PPA's bankability -- including the off-taker's creditworthiness, price escalation mechanisms, and currency denomination -- as part of its cash flow analysis.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/our-approach-to-project-finance
RELATED: Off-Taker, Revenue Stream, Devaluation Risk, Concession Agreement

TERM: Concession Agreement
DEFINITION: A contract between the project company and the host government granting the right to build, own, and operate a project for a specified period. EXIM requires that essential permits, licenses, and concession agreements be in place as part of its host country requirements assessment. The concession must extend beyond the EXIM financing term to provide security for debt repayment.
CONTEXT: The concession agreement establishes the legal basis for the project's existence and operations in the host country. Without a valid concession extending beyond the loan tenor, EXIM cannot take comfort that the project will operate long enough to repay its debt. It is a required data room document.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/our-approach-to-project-finance

TERM: SPV / Project Company (Special Purpose Vehicle)
ACRONYM: SPV
DEFINITION: A newly created legal entity established solely for the purpose of owning and operating a specific project. EXIM lends to project companies and looks to the project's future cash flows as its source of repayment rather than relying on the sponsor's corporate balance sheet. The SPV structure isolates project risk from the sponsor's other business activities.
CONTEXT: The SPV is the borrower entity in EXIM limited-recourse project finance. Its legal structure, jurisdiction, and governance documents are key data room items. EXIM evaluates the SPV's contractual framework (not its balance sheet, since it is purpose-built) to assess creditworthiness.

The review occurs in two phases: Phase I evaluation (initial due diligence, with Preliminary Project Letter issued within 45 days of completion) and Phase II evaluation (final commitment due diligence). The applicant/sponsor pays the financial advisor evaluation fees for both phases.
CONTEXT: EXIM's use of independent financial advisors means sponsors face advisor fees as a significant application cost. For small projects under $30 million, EXIM may waive the financial advisor requirement and rely on internal due diligence and the arranging bank's analysis.

TERM: Liquidated Damages
ACRONYM: LDs
DEFINITION: Pre-agreed monetary penalties specified in the EPC contract payable by the contractor to the project company for failure to meet contractual obligations, typically construction completion delays or performance shortfalls. EXIM requires EPC contracts to include adequate liquidated damages provisions for completion delays, and turnkey construction contracts must include performance guarantees.
CONTEXT: Liquidated damages provide a contractual mechanism to protect the project company (and by extension EXIM) against construction risk. The adequacy of LD provisions is evaluated by EXIM as part of its assessment of risk allocation among project parties.

TERM: Competitive Letter of Interest
ACRONYM: Competitive LI
DEFINITION: A specialized form of Letter of Interest with a higher processing fee ($1,000 versus $100 for standard LI) used when U.S. exporters face competition from foreign exporters backed by their own ECAs. The Competitive LI demonstrates EXIM's willingness to match or counter competing official export credit offers.
CONTEXT: Sponsors and U.S. exporters use Competitive LIs when competing against foreign bids backed by other ECAs. The existence of foreign ECA competition is also relevant to establishing additionality.

TERM: Sponsor Equity
DEFINITION: The capital contributed by project sponsors and strategic equity investors to fund the project's capital costs, cost overruns, and contingent equity requirements. EXIM requires that sponsors commit significant equity, and equity contributions are evaluated as part of the project's risk allocation assessment. The financial model must demonstrate adequate sponsor commitment relative to total project costs.
CONTEXT: EXIM does not publish a fixed minimum equity percentage, but the expectation of "significant" equity means sponsors typically need to contribute 20-30% or more of total project costs. Insufficient equity commitment is a common reason for EXIM application delays or rejection.

TERM: Small Project Finance
DEFINITION: EXIM's streamlined approach for project finance transactions under $30 million. For small projects, EXIM does not require outside legal and financial advisors and may rely instead on internal due diligence and the arranging bank's analysis. This reduces application costs and timeline for smaller transactions while maintaining EXIM's credit standards.
CONTEXT: The $30 million threshold is important for sponsors of smaller infrastructure projects. Below this threshold, the reduced advisory requirements can significantly lower the cost and complexity of the EXIM application process.

TERM: Transformational Export Areas
DEFINITION: Ten industry sectors designated by Congress under CTEP where EXIM can provide enhanced support to counter PRC competition: (1) Artificial Intelligence, (2) Biotechnology, (3) Biomedical Sciences, (4) Wireless Communications (5G), (5) Quantum Computing, (6) Renewable Energy, Storage & Efficiency, (7) Semiconductors, (8) Fintech, (9) Water Treatment & Sanitation, (10) High Performance Computing.
CONTEXT: Projects in these 10 areas can access CTEP's enhanced financing terms including full OECD financing at 51% US content, extended tenors, and policy exceptions. Lodestar should flag when a project falls within a Transformational Export Area.

TERM: Host Government Agreement
ACRONYM: HGA
DEFINITION: An agreement between the project company and the host country government that establishes the legal and regulatory framework for the project, including tax treatment, import/export privileges, dispute resolution mechanisms, and government commitments regarding currency convertibility and availability. EXIM requires evidence of host government commitment, including essential permits, licenses, and concession agreements, as well as evidence of currency convertibility and availability.
CONTEXT: The HGA provides the political and legal foundation for the project in the host country. EXIM evaluates host government support as part of its country risk assessment. A weak or absent HGA increases political risk and may affect EXIM's willingness to provide comprehensive cover.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/our-approach-to-project-finance

TERM: Risk Allocation
DEFINITION: The distribution of project risks among the parties best able to manage them. EXIM evaluates whether risks are appropriately allocated across sponsors, EPC contractors, operators, off-takers, insurers, and host governments. Key risks include construction/completion risk, operating risk, market/price risk, political risk, currency risk, and force majeure.
CONTEXT: Risk allocation is a foundational principle of EXIM project finance underwriting. EXIM will not approve projects where risks are concentrated in parties unable to manage them. The adequacy of risk allocation across the project's contractual framework is a primary credit evaluation criterion.
SOURCE: https://www.exim.gov/solutions/project-and-structured-finance/guidelines-for-submitting-successful-application
